About

We conduct crack surveys to identify the pre-construction condition of the structures before the commencement of a demolition, new construction or development works in nearby areas. In our service all prominent defects of existing structure in the form of cracks, settlement, movement, water seepage, distortion, subsidence and other building defects will be recorded with photographs and details.

Services

Pre-Construction Survey
A Pre-Construction Survey (PCS) or Pre Crack Survey consists of a review of a property adjacent to a site where construction activities are about to begin. The main objective of the survey is to visually identify all existing signs of exterior, interior damage, roof damage or any signs of structural settlement, before construction begins. A PCS can protect the subject property owner and the contractor/developer of the site where construction activities will take place. For a property owner, the PCS documents the existing conditions so that any damage or structural settlement caused by the construction is appropriately attributed to the construction activities and, therefore, the responsibility of the contractor/developer to rectify. From the contractor/developer perspective, the PCS is primarily a loss control and claims defense mechanism. If a property owner alleges that damage was sustained due to construction activities, the pre-construction survey can be referenced to confirm what visible damage pre-existed the construction activity. This PCS document will provide written evidence to both parties to settle the dispute without costly legal proceeding.
Procedure of Pre-Construction Surveys
Prior to the survey, a review of drawings for the proposed adjacent building would be undertaken. This assists in understanding the implication of the proposed work and specific areas at the subject property that should be more closely reviewed and documented. For each crack or anomaly noted, the survey will provide a photograph, the general location of the anomaly, specific location of the anomaly and the anomaly type. if the anomaly is a crack, the size of crack, type of crack and direction of crack will be noted.
Post Crack Survey
Post Crack Surveys should also be undertaken after the construction activities. The purpose of the post crack survey is to document any changes to the previously identified anomalies and identify any new cracks or anomalies in the building structure. Knowing at what point in the construction process a crack occurs, can sometimes aid in identifying the likely cause and therefore implication. For any new anomalies noted, the post crack shall identify the type of anomaly, location and photograph the anomaly. Adjacent owners must exercise extreme due diligence before signing off, to ensure there is no damage to their property.
Public Relationship
If your project has buildings or sensitive structures nearby, get off on the right foot. A solid public relations plan brings peace of mind to you and the building owners in the area. Performing third party existing condition surveys not only provides documentation of existing conditions of structures prior to any piling or construction works, it demonstrates to the home owners that you’re providing a good faith effort to be a good civic partner.

Purpose of a Crack Survey
The purpose of crack surveys is to provide an accurate record of pre-construction and post construction condition of the nearby properties. In the event of a claim for property damage due to vibration, excavation or dewatering, crack survey report will be provided written documentation on the pre-construction condition of the property. Crack survey report will be helpful to claim the damages from insurance and solve the situation an amicable settlement of the dispute with 3rd parties.
